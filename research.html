Research Focus

Computer Science and Blockchain

My research is dedicated to the development and design of smart contracts for building reliable and scalable blockchain applications, with a strong focus on:

  • Modeling business processes on the Algorand blockchain
  • Flexible and modular smart contract engineering
  • Formal verification and validation of smart contracts
  • Security in decentralized application development
  • Performance optimization and scalability of blockchain systems

Current Projects

Flexible Execution of Business processes on Algorand Blockchain.

2023-Present | University of Florence

Creating a component-based architecture for smart contracts that enables safe runtime modifications in business processes, while preserving security guarantees.

Development of Tool for flexible execution of business processes on blockchain.

2024-Present | University of Florence Brunel University London

Developing a framework starting from BPMN choreography diagrams, representing the business interactions between parties, and generates an infrastructure based on a public blockchain. This guarantees at the same time a high level of trust and the flexibility useful to enable changes at run-time.

Smart Contract Translation for Cross-Chain Interoperability

2024-Present | Brunel University London

Exploring the use of Large Language Models (LLMs) to automate the translation of Solidity smart contracts into Algorand's Python-based smart contracts. This project focuses on enabling interoperability between Ethereum and Algorand networks by simplifying contract migration and reducing manual rewriting efforts.
